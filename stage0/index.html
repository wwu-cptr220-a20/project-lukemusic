Worship Planning One-Stop-Shop

Project Overview

Leading a worship band has many more moving pieces and components than people may realize. The leader has to match a myriad of musicians together into one band, taking into account their preferences, musical talent and abilities, schedules, how frequently they’ve played, etc. Then the leader has to pick songs that flow nicely together both in the way they sound and in the message their lyrics convey. Plus, it is best to pick songs that the band enjoys to play. Once the songs and people are chosen, the leader has to find rehearsal times that match everyone’s schedules, reserve a venue to practice in, and find chord charts for the songs. This of course can only be done once the leader knows what keys the singers want the songs in. Then the leader has to arrange each song and decide in what order they want to do the different pieces of the song, taking in to account the way other people usually do the song. Then there comes the matter of managing a rehearsal. The leader has to easily convey to his or her band what they want the songs to sound like. Click tracks are a great tool for this, but these take a long time to make, and a great deal of expertise. Click tracks are audio tracks that have both a metronome and verbal cues that tell the band which piece of the song comes next. Finally, the leader needs to think about communicating their audio needs to a tech team and the lyrics of their songs to the projectionist, and make sure the lyrics are in the perfect order.

There are a myriad of steps that worship leaders have to think about when designing a worship set, and for many the sheer number of steps is daunting. I think it would be great to have a webpage that walks people step-by-step through the process of designing a worship set and has all the necessary tools in one place. Worship leaders typically use countless online tools to accomplish their needs. There are resources for chord charts, song lists, beats per minute for each song, click track creation tutorials, etc. It would be great to have all these tools in one place that are presented at the appropriate steps in the whole process. A worship leader could come to this website and simply walk through every step in the process and have a complete, well polished worship set by the end of it.

Possible App Integrations

  • Song bpm lookup
    • In this app the user could enter the name of literally any song and the bpm for popular recordings of it would be returned. See songbpm.com for an idea of what this would look like. This is great for creating click tracks for songs.
  • Youtube Integration
    • There could be direct youtube playlists embedded in the webpage that would show the user how to do various technical tasks in creating a worship set, like how to create click tracks, how to use planning center, how to see everyone’s schedules in Outlook calendar, etc.
  • Song Key lookup
    • This would be a database of worship songs with suggested keys. The user could enter a song title and possible keys would be returned, like the best key for a guy to sing the song in and the best key for a girl to sing the song in, etc. This is super helpful because then the worship leader can get a jump on song planning without having to wait around for the singers to get back to them.
